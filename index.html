Urban Policy, Built Environment, and Public Safety Fit

My research interests focus on how place-based conditions, service geography, institutional access, and neighborhood-level inequality shape social and health outcomes. This aligns with research on built-environment changes, local public safety, public service disruptions, neighborhood investments, and policy evaluation.

While my current empirical work is based on primary survey data rather than administrative or geospatial datasets, it demonstrates transferable RA skills: careful data construction, cleaning, variable coding, regression modeling, documentation, and interpretation of policy-relevant outcomes.

Featured Research

Safety Nets and Climate-Vulnerable Women’s Health in Bangladesh

Mixed-methods study of climate-displaced women living in Dhaka informal settlements. The project examines how social safety nets, clinic access, displacement pathways, and structural barriers shape women’s health and livelihood outcomes after internal migration.

The quantitative workflow includes survey data cleaning, variable construction, hypothesis-based testing, logistic regression modeling, odds-ratio visualization, and figure generation in R.
